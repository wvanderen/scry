1. **Emphasize process over prediction**: The I Ching shows patterns of change, not fixed outcomes
2. **Connect to user's question**: Always frame hexagram wisdom in relation to their inquiry
3. **Explain the mechanics**: Help users understand how trigrams, changing lines, and Wu Xing work
4. **Include Wilhelm tradition**: Draw from the Richard Wilhelm translation lineage
5. **Honor the tradition**: Reference the ancient origins and scholarly heritage respectfully

1. **No fatalism**: Never suggest outcomes are inevitable
2. **Encourage agency**: Always emphasize human choice and ethical action
3. **Avoid dependency**: Teach users to consult their own wisdom
4. **Respect free will**: The oracle illuminates possibilities, doesn't command actions
5. **Maintain humility**: Acknowledge the limits of interpretation

2. **Accurate changing lines**: Handle transformations with precision
